2. General Rules of Play

  • Black ball placed on the spot when racking up.
  • If the White ball potted on the break, free ball given, taken from behind the line and shot down the table.
  • A minimum of 3 balls must hit the cushion, or at least one ball potted for the break to be legal.
  • Black ball potted off the Break, balls are re‑racked and the same player breaks again.
  • International Rules in place for all other game play.
  • If a player clears all balls from the Break and opponent does not get one shot, the game result is valid.
  • A maximum of 13 players can be chosen from each team to play.
  • A minimum of 6 players from a team must turn up otherwise games will be forfeited.
  • Singles – 3 players = 2 games (4 frames) each + 1 player = 1 game (2 frames).
  • Doubles – 4 singles players plus 2 additional players.
  • Referees and Team Captains to maintain order around the pool table.
  • No coaching allowed.
  • Bar Owners, Team Captains and Players are encouraged to act in the spirit of the league.

3. Competition Format and Scoring system

Singles (7 matches):

  • Best of 2 games
  • Win 2–0 → 3 points per team and per player
  • Draw 1–1 → 1 point per team and per player
  • Maximum 2 matches per player

Doubles (3 matches):

  • 1 game only
  • Each doubles pair must be different
  • Alternating shots
  • Win = 2 points for the team, 1 point per player
  • Doubles scores do not contribute towards a player’s Handicap

Team getting the most points in the competition will get an extra 3 points. If draw between the teams, no extra point.

4. Player Selection Procedure

  • Away Team captain selects the first player for the opening singles match.
  • Home Team captain then selects the opposing player.
  • This alternates for every following singles match.

5. Handicap System

A. Overview

  • Individual handicap system balances competition and rewards consistent performance.
  • Applies only to singles matches.
  • Levels range from 0 (highest) to 3 (lowest).

B. Determining Handicap

  • Based on previous season performance.
  • New players start at Level 1.
  • Adjusted each season based on combined results.
  • Doubles do not contribute to handicap.

C. Handicap Advantage Rules

C.1 Ball Advantage

  • 1 level difference = 1 ball advantage
  • Maximum = 3 balls

C.2 How Advantage Balls Work

  • Player may accept or decline handicap.
  • Referee advises at start of match.
  • Relevant balls removed when handicap applied.

Handicap Brackets

  • 0–40% = 3
  • 41–55% = 2
  • 56–75% = 1
